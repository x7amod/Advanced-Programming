    - createdAt

20. PAYMENT_TRANSACTION

    - receiptNumber

    - createdAt

21. WAITLIST
    - waitlistId (PK)
    - traineeId (FK)
    - sessionId (FK)
    - position
    - addedAt
    - status (Waiting | Offered | Accepted | Expired)
    - expiresAt

22. NOTIFICATION
    - notificationId (PK)
    - recipientUserId (FK)
    - title
    - message
    - type (Enrollment | Schedule | Payment | Assessment | Certification | Reminder | General)
    - relatedEntityType
    - relatedEntityId
    - isRead
    - createdAt
    - readAt

DESIGN DECISIONS - WHY CERTAIN ENTITIES EXIST
================================================================================

📋 WHY SEPARATE ASSESSMENT ENTITY?
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Instead of storing pass/fail directly on the Enrollment entity, we use a 
separate Assessment entity for these reasons:

1. TEMPORAL DECOUPLING: Enrollment happens at registration time, but 
   assessment occurs after the course session ends. They are different 
   business events occurring at different times.

2. AUDIT TRAIL: Assessment records WHO (instructor) evaluated WHEN and with 
   what result. This provides accountability and traceability for academic 
   records.

3. NULLABLE vs REQUIRED: Not all enrollments have assessments (student may 
   drop before completion). A separate entity makes this optional relationship 
   explicit rather than having nullable fields on Enrollment.

4. HISTORICAL DATA: If assessment methods change or re-evaluation is needed, 
   separate records preserve the history without complicating the enrollment 
   lifecycle.

5. INSTRUCTOR ACCOUNTABILITY: Links assessment directly to the instructor who 
   evaluated the trainee, important for quality control and dispute resolution.

6. EXTENSIBILITY: Future enhancements (graded assessments, multiple assessment 
   types, rubrics, score breakdowns) are easier with a dedicated entity.


⏳ WHY WAITLIST SUPPORT?
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Waitlist is essential for managing course demand and maximizing revenue:

1. CAPACITY MANAGEMENT: When a session reaches maxCapacity, interested trainees 
   can join a waitlist rather than being turned away completely.

2. AUTOMATIC BACKFILL: If an enrolled trainee drops or cancels, the system can 
   automatically offer the spot to the next person on the waitlist (by position).

3. BUSINESS CONTINUITY: Prevents lost revenue from cancellations. Instead of 
   empty seats, waitlisted trainees can be promoted.

4. DEMAND FORECASTING: Waitlist size indicates demand, helping coordinators 
   decide whether to schedule additional sessions for popular courses.

5. FAIR ALLOCATION: Position-based ordering ensures first-come, first-served 
   fairness. Status tracking (Waiting → Offered → Accepted/Expired) manages 
   the promotion workflow.

6. TIME-BOUND OFFERS: expiresAt allows offering a spot with a deadline. If not 
   accepted in time, the offer moves to the next person automatically.

7. TRAINEE EXPERIENCE: Better UX than "sorry, course is full" - trainees have 
   hope and get notified when spots open up.


💰 WHY UNIFIED PAYMENT TRACKING?
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
We use PaymentRecord (master) + PaymentTransaction (detail) pattern:

1. PAYMENT_RECORD: One per enrollment, tracks overall billing status (total due, 
   total paid, balance, status). This is the "account" view.

2. PAYMENT_TRANSACTION: Multiple transactions per record, captures each individual 
   payment event (partial payments over time, different payment methods).

3. FLEXIBILITY: Trainees can pay in installments. Each payment is logged as a 
   transaction, and paidAmount is updated cumulatively on the record.

4. AUDIT TRAIL: Every payment transaction includes date, method, receipt number, 
   and who recorded it (coordinator).

5. OVERDUE TRACKING: The PaymentRecord status automatically reflects whether 
   payment is overdue based on dueDate and balanceDue.
